Flagship · anomaly detection

amsg-pipeline

A telescope for signals that don't announce themselves.

A CPU-only Python pipeline that searches worldwide neutron-monitor data for moments when many independent instruments deviate together during quiet solar wind — assuming no template of the source, only its footprint. A null result becomes a calibrated upper limit by injecting known signals and measuring what would have been caught. It grew into a cross-network search across three unrelated physics — neutron monitors, INTERMAGNET magnetometers, and GNSS TEC — so a real candidate has to show up in all three at once, and false alarms die by multiplied rarity.

◆ Two solar minima + two cross-network years scanned — all-zero, every real event attributed by name. Validated on the GLE72 ground-level enhancement (2017) and Forbush decreases · ~15 modular CLI stages · 97 tests · connectors to NMDB, INTERMAGNET, CEDAR Madrigal, OMNI/HAPI, NOAA SWPC, USGS.
